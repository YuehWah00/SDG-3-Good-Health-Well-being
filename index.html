What is SDG 3

SDG 3 aims to ensure everyone can live a healthy life by reducing diseases, improving healthcare access, and promoting physical and mental well-being. It focuses on prevention, early treatment, healthy lifestyle habits, and creating safe environments that support long-term health for all ages.

Malaysia Health Overview

Malaysia faces growing health challenges from lifestyle-related non- communicable diseases, with heart disease, diabetes, cancer, and stroke as leading causes. Poor diet, inactivity, and smoking drive these trends, though strong healthcare coverage, high immunization rates, and national programs continue to support healthier living.

Our Mission

Our mission is to advance public health and make well-being a way of life. By reducing preventable diseases, strengthening healthcare access, and empowering Malaysians through education, innovation, and community engagement, we support SDG 3 and inspire healthier choices. Together, we can shape a healthier future for our nation.

Wellness Tips

Healthy Eating

  • Choose more fruits, vegetables, whole grains and lean proteins.
  • Reduce sugar, salt and oily food.
  • Drink at least 6–8 glasses of water a day.
  • Practice portion control, eat until 80% full.
  • Avoid skipping breakfast to maintain energy and focus.

Exercise & Fitness

  • Do 30 minutes of physical activity daily.
  • Combine cardio and strength training.
  • Stretch to improve flexibility and prevent injury.
  • Take short movement breaks during long sitting.
  • Choose activities you enjoy to stay consistent.

Mental Health Care

  • Sleep 7–9 hours every night.
  • Practice mindfulness or meditation.
  • Stay connected with supportive friends and family.
  • Limit screen time before bed.
  • Seek help early if overwhelmed.

Daily Healthy Habits

  • Keep a regular sleep schedule.
  • Maintain good hygiene and wash hands often.
  • Stay hydrated and carry a water bottle.
  • Plan your day to reduce stress.
  • Make small, consistent habits.
